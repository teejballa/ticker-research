## Overview

v1.0 delivered Cipher — a deployable, self-calibrating financial research assistant. The user enters a ticker, Cipher gathers multi-source data (yahoo + finnhub + polygon market data, news/filings/analyst content via Anthropic web search, StockTwits + options sentiment, Firecrawl-scraped community intelligence, Yahoo + EDGAR institutional/insider flows), reasons over it via Gemini through the Vercel AI Gateway with a Zod-validated schema, and produces a structured report grounded in retrieved sources.

The signature innovation is the **Diffusion Learning Engine**: three Vercel cron jobs (sentiment-scan → price-followup → learn) close a Bayesian feedback loop. Every research signal (diffusion sentiment, technical pattern, institutional 13F flow, insider Form 4 cluster) is captured at scan time, the ticker's price move at 3/7/14/30/60/90 days vs SPY is recorded, and a Beta posterior per `(signal_class × pattern_key × cap_class × horizon_days)` cell is updated. At report time, the matching prior is injected into the Gemini prompt as an Engine Calibration block — so reports reason from learned alpha-vs-SPY evidence, not generic logic. As of milestone close: 18 LearnedPattern cells with observations, 2 already ACTIVE (calibrated), 87 PriceOutcome rows, 70 LearningEvents.

The architecture pivoted decisively in **Phase 12** when the original Python/NotebookLM/container reasoning layer was replaced with a pure-TypeScript Gemini pipeline — eliminating cross-process latency, container infrastructure, and a third-party reasoning dependency.

**Key Decisions:**
- **Gemini via Vercel AI Gateway** (Phase 12) — eliminated provider key, container, and Python from the reasoning path; unified billing
- **Field-level multi-source merge** (Phase 10) — partial yahoo data is supplemented by finnhub/polygon per-field rather than replaced wholesale
- **Bayesian Beta priors per cell** (Phase 15) — each `(signal_class × pattern_key × cap_class × horizon_days)` cell maintains its own α/β posterior
- **Multi-horizon outcomes** (Phase 16) — outcomes resolve at 3/7/14/30/60/90d, with 30d as the primary horizon for the 12-d logistic regression
- **Yahoo-primary + real EDGAR fallback** (Phase 17) — yahoo provides broad cheap coverage, EDGAR is authoritative for the long tail
- **NextAuth (Google) for identity** — single-provider OAuth, Report.user_id scopes per-user history
- **Decommission containers** (Phase 12) — pure-TypeScript pipeline runs on Vercel Functions/Crons; no separate infrastructure
